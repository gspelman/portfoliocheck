About

The City of Ann Arbor is a government entity of the state of Michigan, made up of over forty departments that support various community needs. Related services it provides include but is not limited to: animals and pets, arts, business, community, education, environment, human services, parks and recreation, safety and court, transportation, and utilities. The City of Ann Arbor website is its primary repository of services and information intended for the city’s residents, visitors, businesses, and employees. Scope and Objectives

Scope

The scope of this project includes the desktop and mobile versions of the city website, in particular the website homepage and the Parks and Recreation page. The objectives were to:
1. Provide recommendations to improve the website’s information architecture.
2. Research methods for making the website’s information more engaging and relatable to its users.
3. Write a transparent report that gives the city resources to re-evaluate their website again in the future.

Questions

From initial discussions with the client, the opportunity statement was framed as follows: Due to the organization and amount of information on the City of Ann Arbor’s website, users may be having issues finding relevant information for their needs.

Consequently, these questions were outlined as targets for research:
1.What are the key goals or motivations of website users?
2. Are users successful in achieving those goals? Why or why not?
2a. How do users go about finding specific information?
2b. What do users like about the website? What do they not like?
2c. How does the website rank as a source of local information? If users prefer other channels, why?
